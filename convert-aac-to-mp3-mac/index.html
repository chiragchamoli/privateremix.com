FAQ

AAC to MP3, answered.

Will I lose quality converting AAC to MP3?

The change is minimal. Both formats are compressed, and PrivateRemix converts at a high quality setting so there is no visible or audible drop for everyday use.

Is AAC better than MP3?

AAC is slightly more efficient at the same bitrate, but MP3 wins on universal compatibility, which is why people convert to it.

Does every device play MP3?

Effectively yes. MP3 is the most widely supported audio format in the world, which is why it's the go-to for sharing.

Do my files get uploaded when I convert AAC to MP3?

No. PrivateRemix runs entirely on your Mac using its own processor. Your AAC files never leave the device — there is no server to upload to, and it works fully offline.

Can I convert several AAC files to MP3 at once?

Yes. Drop in a batch of AAC files and PrivateRemix converts them all locally, saving each new MP3 next to its original.
