FAQ

SVG to JPG, answered.

Will I lose quality converting SVG to JPG?

The change is minimal. Both formats are compressed, and you choose the size-versus-quality balance — Smaller file, Balanced, or Best quality — so everyday use shows no visible or audible drop.

Can any image become a clean SVG?

Vector tracing works best on logos, icons and flat graphics with clear edges. Detailed photographs will trace, but the result is an approximation rather than a pixel-perfect copy.

Is JPG good for photos?

Yes. JPG is the most widely supported photo format and compresses photographs efficiently. It is a lossy format, so it is best for final images rather than repeated re-editing.

Do my files get uploaded when I convert SVG to JPG?

No. PrivateRemix runs entirely on your Mac using its own processor. Your SVG files never leave the device — there is no server to upload to, and it works fully offline.

Can I convert several SVG files to JPG at once?

Yes. Drop in a batch of SVG files and PrivateRemix converts them all locally, saving each new JPG next to its original.
